Master QC 1000 Implementation
for Your Audit Firm

Expert panel discussion with former PCAOB Director and former PwC Chief Auditor

As the December 15, 2026 effective date for PCAOB's QC 1000 approaches, many audit leaders at registered firms are navigating a complex set of implementation questions with significant stakes. This course presents a recorded expert panel discussion in which three practitioners with direct experience at the intersection of PCAOB regulation and firm-level quality management address the most pressing questions firms are facing today.

The panel examines the key structural differences between SQMS No. 1 — the AICPA's quality management standard — and QC 1000, evaluates what implementation actually demands of firms at different scales, and directly dispels common misconceptions that are creating unnecessary confusion in the field. This is a practical, experience-driven conversation designed specifically for audit professionals responsible for getting their firm's quality control system right.

Learning Objectives

Upon successful completion of this course, participants will be able to:

  1. Recognize the annual reporting obligation unique to QC 1000 — including the Form QC filing requirement — that distinguishes it from SQMS No. 1 and predecessor standards.
  2. Identify the risk-based approach required under QC 1000, including how firms must assess quality risks and design corresponding policies and procedures as responses.
  3. Evaluate the post-implementation obligations under QC 1000, including ongoing monitoring, remediation of deficiencies, and the annual evaluation requirement.

QC 1000 — PCAOB's quality control standard, effective December 15, 2026, replacing QC 20 and QC 25.

SQMS No. 1 — The AICPA's quality management standard for non-PCAOB-registered firms, effective December 15, 2025.

PCAOB — Public Company Accounting Oversight Board; oversees audits of public companies and SEC-registered brokers/dealers.

QC 20 / QC 25 — PCAOB's predecessor quality control standards superseded by QC 1000.

Form QC — Annual filing unique to QC 1000 in which PCAOB-registered firms attest to their quality control system design, monitoring results, and self-reported deficiencies.

Quality Control System — A firm's integrated set of policies, procedures, and responses designed to provide reasonable assurance that the firm and its personnel comply with professional standards.

Reasonable Assurance — A high, but not absolute, level of assurance; the standard of assurance QC 1000 requires of a firm's quality control system.

Quality Objectives — Firm-level goals that define what the quality control system is designed to achieve; the basis against which quality risks are identified.

Quality Risks — Threats to the achievement of a firm's quality objectives; identified through the firm's risk assessment process.

Risk Assessment Process — The process by which a firm identifies quality risks and designs responses to address them.

Monitoring — Ongoing evaluation of whether the firm's quality control system is suitably designed and operating effectively.

Remediation — Corrective action taken to address deficiencies identified through the monitoring process.

Engagement Quality Review (EQR) — An objective evaluation of significant judgments made by the engagement team before the report is issued.

Annual Evaluation — The annual assessment required under QC 1000 in which a firm evaluates whether its quality control system is suitably designed and operating effectively, with results communicated to the PCAOB.
